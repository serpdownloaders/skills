## Why Adobe Stock Downloader

Adobe Stock hosts millions of assets across categories like photography, vector graphics, video footage, and design templates. When you license an asset, downloading it still requires several clicks through the Adobe Stock interface, and retrieving previously licensed files means digging through your license history page by page. There is no built-in way to grab multiple licensed assets at once or to automatically organize files as they land on your hard drive.

Adobe Stock Downloader is being built to sit inside the browser and interact with the Adobe Stock website directly. The goal is to recognize which assets on the current page you have already licensed, surface a download control on each one, and handle file retrieval in the background so you can keep browsing while your assets save locally.

## Planned Features

- One-click download of any licensed Adobe Stock asset visible on the page
- Batch retrieval from your license history without opening each asset individually
- Automatic file naming using the asset title, ID, and contributor metadata
- Support for all Adobe Stock asset types including photos, vectors, videos, and templates
- Resolution and format selection when multiple options are available for a licensed asset
- Download queue that processes assets in the background while you continue browsing
- Browser-native workflow that does not require Adobe Creative Cloud desktop apps
- Cross-browser compatibility targeting Chrome, Edge, Brave, and Firefox

## How It Will Work

1. Install the extension once it is released from the browser extension store.
2. Navigate to Adobe Stock in your browser and sign in to your Adobe account.
3. Browse or search for assets as you normally would on the Adobe Stock site.
4. Licensed assets on the page will be detected and marked by the extension.
5. Click the download button on any licensed asset to save it immediately.
6. Open the extension popup to access your license history for batch downloads.
7. Select the resolution or format variant you prefer if the asset offers multiple options.
8. Files save to your designated local folder with metadata-based filenames.

## Expected Formats

- Photos: JPEG, PNG, or TIFF depending on the licensed resolution and asset type
- Vectors: AI, EPS, or SVG as provided by the original contributor upload
- Videos: MOV or MP4 at the licensed resolution, typically up to 4K
- Templates: PSD, AI, or INDD files depending on the template category

Downloaded files will match the formats Adobe Stock delivers for each asset type. No conversion is applied unless explicitly selected by the user.

## FAQ

**When will Adobe Stock Downloader be released?**
A release date has not been set. Sign up at the waitlist link above to be notified as soon as it is available.

**Does it download watermarked preview assets?**
No. This extension is designed to work with assets you have already licensed through your Adobe Stock subscription or credit purchases. It retrieves the full licensed file, not the watermarked preview.

**Will it require Adobe Creative Cloud to be installed?**
No. The extension operates entirely within the browser and communicates with the Adobe Stock website directly. You do not need the Creative Cloud desktop application installed.

**What about assets licensed under an Adobe Stock team account?**
The extension will work with whatever assets are accessible under the Adobe account you are signed into in the browser, including team and enterprise accounts.

**Is it free?**
Pricing details will be announced closer to launch. SERP extensions typically include a free trial period.

**Can I download assets I have not licensed?**
No. The extension only facilitates downloading assets tied to valid licenses on your account. It does not bypass Adobe Stock licensing.

## About Adobe Stock

Adobe Stock is Adobe's integrated stock content marketplace, offering over 300 million assets including photos, vectors, illustrations, videos, templates, and 3D content. It connects directly into Creative Cloud applications like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro. Users acquire content through credit-based purchases or monthly subscriptions. Adobe Stock Downloader is being built to give users a faster browser-based path to retrieve the licensed assets they have already paid for, without relying on the Creative Cloud desktop workflow.
